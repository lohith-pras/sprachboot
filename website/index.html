Voice & chat

Talk, don’t study.

Hold the mic and just speak. Broken German is the whole point — SprachBoot replies in German at your level and keeps the conversation moving. Type instead if you’d rather. Either way, you’re practising the thing you actually need: talking.

One loop, getting smarter.

Every turn teaches SprachBoot something about how you speak. The next turn uses it.

01

You speak.

Voice or text, in whatever German you’ve got. Messy is fine.

02

It replies in German.

Short, natural, at your level — and switches to English for one quick note only when something really breaks.

03

Your mistakes get analysed.

Word order, gender, case, false friends — sorted by what trips you up most, quietly in the background.

04

Your profile updates.

Confident words, weak patterns, words due for review — all kept so the next conversation can lean on them.

05

The next session is easier.

It weaves in what you nearly forgot and gently presses on what you’re still getting wrong.

0 months — the plan

A1 to B1, about fifteen minutes a day.

That’s the target SprachBoot is built around: conversational B1 in roughly six months of daily practice. No grammar tables, no listening drills — just talking, a bit every day. Your real progress is whatever you put in.
